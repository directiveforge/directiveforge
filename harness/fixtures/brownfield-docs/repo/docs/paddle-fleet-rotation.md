# Paddle-fleet rotation

Governs how paddle gear (kayaks, boards, drysuits, PFDs) moves through the summer-heavy
depots (Stonebrook and Pinecrest) over the summer peak. Paddle gear wears fast and unevenly,
so rotation is deliberate.

## Why rotation matters

A kayak rented every day for a month is a kayak retired early. Rotating rentals across the
fleet evens out wear and keeps replacement spend predictable. Uneven wear is the enemy.

## Rotation rule

Each paddle item carries a rental-count tag. The floor rents the lowest-count in-condition
item first, not the nearest one on the rack. This spreads wear across the fleet.

## Drysuit note

Drysuits are the highest-maintenance paddle line and are under active supplier review — see
the archived vendor comparison at the repo root. No supplier decision is locked yet; hold
until summer numbers are in (master-plan roadmap).

## Mid-season inspection

Halfway through the summer peak (mid-July), every paddle item gets an off-water inspection
per `inventory-cycle-count.md`. Anything failing goes to repair, not back on the rack.

## Deposits

Paddle deposits follow the network replacement-cost rule (D2). Drysuits size high; PFDs
size low. No flat fees.
