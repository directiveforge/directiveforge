## Methodology note

Fresh-context adversarial sweep with no access to the fixture answer key (`FIXTURE-CARD.md` and all of `harness/fixtures/brownfield-docs/` except the pristine `repo/` source tree were treated as off-limits). I read the pristine source in full first to establish ground truth, then read every generated file, then ran each generated gate/command mechanically against the live generated tree to falsify — not merely eyeball — each candidate. Verification included: diffing all 13 pre-existing files (playbooks, INDEX, governance, HANDOFF, master plan) to confirm they were left byte-identical (they were); executing the generator's own link-health/index-drift/ledger scripts to see what they actually emit; grepping for `{{…}}` residue, phantom paths, and cross-file fact drift; and comparing the merged CLAUDE.md against its own `.backup` to catch dropped hand-authored rules. Pre-existing fixture-planted defects (2 dead INDEX rows, 2 stale `verified:` dates) were explicitly excluded from the generator's score since the generator correctly surfaced-but-did-not-edit them per evolve-scope. The flagship finding (C1) is CONFIRMED by direct execution, not inference: the primary blocking gate emits 13 dead-link lines on a corpus with 2 real dead links, making it worse than useless. Reviewer-variance caveat: F1's severity hinges on whether the operator approved the archive move (unknowable without the answer key) — filed ⚠ on the falsifiable half (a hand-authored rule was dropped while PHASE9 claimed full preservation), independent of the approval question.
