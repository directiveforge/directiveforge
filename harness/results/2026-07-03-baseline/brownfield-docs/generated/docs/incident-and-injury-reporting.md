# Incident and injury reporting

Renting outdoor gear means the gear goes into the backcountry with customers. When
something goes wrong with a rented item, this is how a depot handles and records it.

## What counts as an incident

- A rented item failing in the field (a snapped strap, a leaking drysuit, a released
  binding).
- A customer reporting an injury they attribute to a rented item.
- Any near-miss a customer volunteers.

## Immediate handling

1. **Customer safety first.** If anyone is hurt, direct them to care before any paperwork.
2. **Pull the item.** The specific rented item comes off the fleet immediately, tagged, and
   is not re-rented until inspected. Do not clean or repair it before inspection — it may
   need to be examined as-returned.
3. **Log it same day.** Record item, depot, date, what happened, and customer account.

## Escalation

Every injury-linked incident is escalated to the founder same day, not batched. Field
failures without injury are logged and reviewed at the weekly review ritual.

## Fleet-wide check

If an incident points to a defect class rather than a one-off (e.g. a strap type failing
across multiple items), the whole affected line gets an immediate condition pass via
`inventory-cycle-count.md`, ahead of its normal cycle.

## What not to do

- Do not re-rent an item involved in an injury report until it is inspected and cleared.
- Do not resolve an injury incident on the floor without founder awareness.
